Easy Chicken Pot Pie with Buttery Herb Biscuit

Description

What could be more traditional of a fall/winter meal than a pot pie? This chicken pot pie is loaded with a creamy filling, made up of yummy veggie and chicken goodness. The best part about pot this pot pie recipe lies in the crust. Instead of your typical standard pie crust, we mixed it up by topping this dish with buttery herb biscuits. This is the ultimate comfort classic, and you're guaranteed to love it!

Ingredients

For Pot Pie Filling
  • 4 T unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 medium russet potato, peeled & diced
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped or grated
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 10 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 8 fresh sage leaves
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • kosher salt & ground black pepper, to season
For Biscuit Topping:
  • one 16.3-ounce can extra flaky biscuits (from refrigerator section)
  • 2 T unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 T finely chopped fresh herbs of choice
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped or grated
  • flaky sea salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375-degrees F. Gather & prep all ingredients according to the Ingredients List, above.
  2. Cook chicken: Add 2 T butter to a 12-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Season chicken breasts with salt/pepper. Place chicken in the skillet to brown for ~2-3 min/side. Transfer the skillet to the oven for the chicken to roast for 15-20 minutes. Set aside to cool slightly. Once cool enough to handle, shred chen into bite-sized pieces using a fork.
  3. Cook aromatics: Place skillet back over medium heat and add the remaining 2 T of butter. Once melted, add in: onion, carrots, celery, and potato. Season with salt/pepper. Stir to combine. Cook for 5-7 min, stirring occasionally, until the veggies are softened and slightly browned. Add the garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  4. Make sauce: Sprinkle flour over softened veggies and stir to combine. While scraping the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon, slowly pour in the chicken stock. It will bubble vigorously, almost cooking all the way down very quickly. Be sure to scrape up any brown bit that may have formed at the bottom of the skillet - that's where the flavor is! While stirring constantly, slowly pour in milk. Once combined, add: rosemary, thyme, sage. Bring to a simmer and cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened slightly. Add salt/pepper to taste.
  5. Finish pot pie: Stir the shredded chicken and frozen peas into the pot pie filling. Arrange the flaky biscuits over top. In a small bowl, mix melted butter with the chopped herbs and garlic. Brush this buttery goodness mixture over the biscuits. Finish with a sprinkle of salt, if desired.
  6. Bake pot pie: Carefully transfer your heavy, full skillet to the oven. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the biscuits are golden & cooked through. Carefully remove from the oven and cool slightly.
  7. Serve! Removing any spent herb stems from the skillet, portion the chicken pot pie into individual bowls. Serve and enjoy:)
